About Us

Building the UK's ISRU Community

ISRU UK is a collaborative, non-commercial network that brings together organisations and individuals across the United Kingdom who are active in the field of In-Situ Resource Utilisation.

Formed in 2023 at the request of the UK Space Agency — following ISRU being identified as a priority area in the 2021 National Space Strategy and the 2023 Space Exploration Technology Roadmap — the group operates independently of UKSA and represents the UK ISRU community as a whole.

We connect researchers, engineers, policymakers, and industry professionals to foster innovation, advise government, and raise the profile of UK ISRU capability internationally.

Our Purpose

What We're Here to Do

ISRU UK exists to serve the UK space community through eight core objectives.

01

Grow the Network

Expand membership to include as many UK organisations and individuals active in the ISRU technology sector as possible.

02

Advise Government

Counsel the UK Space Agency and other governmental bodies on matters relating to ISRU policy, strategy, and capability.

03

Foster Innovation

Promote collaborative innovation across the domestic ISRU ecosystem, facilitating partnerships between industry, academia, and government.

04

International Profile

Raise the profile of UK ISRU capability on the international stage through engagement with space agencies and international forums.

05

Share Knowledge

Communicate relevant information to industry, academia, space agencies, educational communities, and all those with an interest in ISRU.

06

Maximise Benefits

Identify and promote the benefits of ISRU technologies — in space exploration and in terrestrial applications.

07

Public Outreach

Develop and pursue outreach opportunities to engage the UK general public with ISRU science and technology.

08

Referral Point

Serve as the primary point of contact for ISRU expertise in the UK, connecting enquirers with the right people.

Governance

How We're Organised

ISRU UK is governed by a rolling Management Committee elected from the community. Leadership transitions through a structured progression, ensuring continuity while bringing in fresh perspectives.

Decisions are taken by consensus wherever possible, falling back to simple majority when needed. Meetings are held six times per year and are open to the full community.

Co-Chair B → Co-Chair A → Chair → Chair Emeritus
